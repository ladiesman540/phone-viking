Contacts

These are the people the system can dispatch to when a job comes in. There are two types: techs (the people who actually show up and do the work) and partners (companies you subcontract to). When a routing rule fires, it picks from this list based on area, availability, and priority tier.

Roster

All Contacts

Each contact needs at minimum a name, phone number, and at least one service area to be dispatchable. The priority tier controls who gets called first (tier 1 = first choice). Mark contacts inactive to temporarily remove them from dispatch without deleting them.

Routing

This is the brain of the dispatch system. It controls three things: what the AI agent asks the caller, what messages get sent out, and the rules that decide who gets called. Here's the flow: caller phones in → agent collects info using the fields below → system matches a routing rule → dispatches to the right contacts via call/SMS → posts a summary to Slack.

Step 1

What the Agent Asks

These are the questions the AI phone agent asks the caller. Each field becomes a question in the conversation — things like "What's your name?", "What's the issue?", "What's the address?" The agent uses these answers to fill in the job and match a routing rule.

Step 2

What Gets Sent Out

After a job is created, the system sends messages to your team and contacts. These templates control the exact wording. You can use tokens like {{callerName}} or {{issueType}} and they'll be replaced with the real values from the job.

Available tokens: {{jobId}}, {{callerName}}, {{callbackNumber}}, {{serviceAddress}}, {{locationArea}}, {{issueType}}, {{urgency}}, {{summary}}, {{businessName}}, {{contactName}}

Step 3

Who Gets Called

Routing rules are if/then logic. Each rule says: "If the issue type is X, and the area is Y, and it's during Z hours — then call these contacts, in this order, with this escalation strategy." Rules are checked top to bottom by sort order. The first one that matches wins.

Setup

Connect the services that make Phone Viking work. The system needs three things: Twilio to make phone calls and send texts, Vapi to power the AI voice agents, and Slack to notify your team. Set these up once and you shouldn't need to touch them again.

Your Business

Workspace

Basic info about your business. The timezone and business hours are used by routing rules — you can create rules that only fire during business hours or only after hours.

Twilio

Phone Calls and Texts

Twilio is how the system makes outbound calls to techs and sends SMS messages. You need two phone numbers from your Twilio account — one for voice calls, one for texts (they can be the same number). Sensitive fields render blank after refresh; leaving them blank on save preserves the stored values.

Vapi

Voice Agent

Vapi powers the AI voice agents. This dashboard stores the outbound dispatch assistant configuration used to call techs and callers back. Sensitive fields render blank after refresh; leaving them blank on save preserves the stored values.

Slack

Team Notifications

When a new job comes in, the system posts a summary to Slack so your team can see it in real time. You can use either an incoming webhook or a bot token plus channel ID. Sensitive fields render blank after refresh; leaving them blank on save preserves the stored values.
